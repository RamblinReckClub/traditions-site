Irina Falconi

Former Player for Tech's Women's Tennis Team

Irina Falconi will always be remembered as an ace within the Georgia Tech Tennis history books. She was born in Portoviejo, Ecuador but grew up in Manhattan, New York. She played for Georgia Tech for two years before making the decision in 2010 to pursue her career in professional tennis. She ended her Georgia Tech career ranked #1 nationally after having a 40-3 singles record her last season. She holds the school record with 26 dual match victories and was named the All-ACC Player of the Year in 2010.

Since leaving Tech, she has played in the Wimbledon, US Open, Australian Open, and the Roland Garros. She also won the Abierto Tampico Tournament in 2017. Irina set records for Georgia Tech, was dominant in the college tennis scene, and she continues to carry on that legacy of success in the professional arena.
