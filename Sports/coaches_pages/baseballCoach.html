Danny Hall

Head coach of Tech's Baseball Team

Danny Hall is one of the longest tenured coaches at Georgia Tech. The 2020 season marked Hallâ€™s 27th season as the head coach of the baseball team. Drafted in the 12th round by the Oakland Athletics, he decided instead to play baseball at Miami University. He spent some time there as a graduate assistant before landing an assistant coach role at Michigan where he spent seven years.

His first head coaching job came at Kent State where he spent another five years. In his time at Kent State, Hall led the team to two Mid American Conference (MAC) Championships and won MAC Coach of the Year in those same years. In 1994, Danny Hall became the head coach for the Yellow Jackets.

In his first year at Tech, he managed to lead the team of veterans to a College World Series where they finished second. Following his first year, he managed to lead the baseball team to two more College World Series. These three appearances mark the only College World Series appearances for Tech.

He has continued to have immense success with the Jackets, boasting strong offensive talent by finishing first or second in the ACC in batting or run production in 15 of his 26 years at Tech. Danny Hall has also had 126 players drafted 143 times with 14 of those being first round picks. He has also led the Jackets to five ACC Tournament Championships and six ACC Regular Season Championships. Danny Hall has an incredible book of accolades in his time at Tech and he will continue to the lead the Jackets in the coming seasons.
