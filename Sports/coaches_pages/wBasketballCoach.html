Nell Fortner

Head coach of Tech's Women's Basketball Team

Nell Fortner is one of the winningest coaches in women’s basketball history with an overall record of 305-187 (.620) across all levels. She began her collegiate coaching career at Stephen F. Austin (SFA) in 1986 as a graduate assistant. She spent three more seasons at SFA guiding them to three NCAA Tournament appearances. She then moved to Louisiana Tech as an assistant coach for five more seasons boasting a .794 record and five NCAA Tournament appearances.

She also began her international coaching career with the US National Team as an assistant coach in 1995 before landing her first head coaching position at Purdue. At Purdue, she led the team to a Big Ten Conference Championship winning Basketball Times’ Coach of the Year, but she left shortly after to become the head coach for the US National Team. In her time at the helm for the USA team, she went on to win gold in the 2000 Olympics as well as winning the 1998 World Championship.

Fortner then went on to spend eight years at Auburn where she had three 20+ win seasons and an SEC Conference Championship. In 2019, Nell Fortner joined Georgia Tech as its sixth women’s basketball head coach. In her first year, she led the team to a 20-11 overall record before falling to eventual ACC Champions, NC State, in the quarterfinals of the ACC Tournament.
