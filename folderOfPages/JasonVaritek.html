T

Jason Varitek

Former Catcher for Tech Baseball

Georgia Tech has produced some incredible baseball players in its time, but none have been as successful or prolific as Tech giant Jason Varitek. Lovingly called “Tek,” he saw success at all levels of baseball, from the Little League, college, to professional arenas. Tek helped lead his Little League team from Altamonte Springs, Florida, to the 1984 Little League World Series, which they won 4-2 over Southport, Indiana. In high school, he was a third baseman and relief pitcher, but he became Georgia Tech’s catcher from 1991 to 1994.

During his time at Tech, the team went to the College World Series in 1994, but they ultimately lost to the University of Oklahoma. He still holds records at Tech for most games played, runs scored, hits, and doubles. His other awards include the Dick Howser Award, given to the best collegiate player of the year, and the Golden Spikes Award.

He went on to play for the Boston Red Sox as part of the 2004 and 2007 World Series Championship teams. He was named the captain, being only the fourth Red Sox captain since 1923. In 2012 he announced his retirement from Major League Baseball and became a special assistant for the Boston Red Sox.
