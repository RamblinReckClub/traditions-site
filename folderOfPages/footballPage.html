Geoff Collins continues to lead the transition from triple option football into a more pro-style offense in his second year here at the Flats. While his first year did not lead to the best record, the potential for growth and the 26th best recruiting class provides hope for a bright future. Collins will try to cement his legacy along with legendary Tech coaches of the past such as John Heisman, the namesake of the award for the College Football Player of the Year, and Bobby Dodd, the namesake for the College Football Coach of the Year.

Tech has been historically successful with four National Championships (more than U[sic]GA, don’t let them forget that) and 15 Conference Championships since the team’s first year in 1892. The Jackets play their home games at Bobby Dodd Stadium at Historic Grant Field, the oldest on-campus site in the sport, where they once defeated Cumberland College 222-0 in 1916. However, for this season and until 2024, Tech will have the opportunity to play a home game against Notre Dame at Mercedes-Benz Stadium, home of the Atlanta Falcons. In addition to that game, Tech will host Clemson, Gardner-Webb, UCF, Virginia, Duke, and Miami this year in their quest to win a bowl game for the first time since 2016.
