Courtney Shealy Hart

Head coach of Tech's Swimming Team

Courtney Shealy Hart is a two-time Olympic gold medalist, a five-time NCAA champion, and was the 2000 NCAA Women’s Swimmer of the Year. She is from Irmo, South Carolina and swam for Athens Community College, leading them to remarkable back to back NCAA Championships. She has been leading the Jackets since 2009 and is the eighth head coach in the history of the program.

In her time as head coach, she has led the team to 39 NCAA participants, five NCAA All-Americans, and 17 ACC Champions. Hart participated in the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney, Australia, where she took home gold in the 400 meter medley relay and the 400 meter freestyle relay. Under her instruction, Georgia Tech swimming has also been one of the most accomplished teams academically. Both the men’s and women’s teams have annually earned All-American Scholar awards.
