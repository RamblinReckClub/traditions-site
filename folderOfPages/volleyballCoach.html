Michelle Collier

Head coach of the Tech Volleyball Team

Michelle Collier, a native of Brazil, attended the University of South Florida (USF) where she became one of the greatest-collegiate volleyball players in the nation, ranking fifth in NCAA Division I history with 2,729 career kills. Collier’s number is the only jersey number to have been retired among USF’s volleyball players. After graduating from USF, she went on to play professional volleyball in Indonesia, Spain, the Netherlands, Cyprus, and Puerto Rico where she won the Indonesian League MVP and Cyprus’s Best Foreign Player.

In 2008, Collier returned to USF as an assistant coach where she was responsible for outside hitters and international recruitment. She had another brief stint at the University of South Carolina as an assistant coach before finally becoming the head coach at Jacksonville State University (JSU). Her JSU team swept the Atlantic Sun Conference and postseason in 2013. With a 30-4 overall record at JSU, Collier went on to win the Atlantic Sun Conference Coach of the Year.

In 2014, she became the head coach at Georgia Tech where she has seen considerable success. In 2019, with a 21-8 record, she led the Jackets to their first NIVC and a second-place finish in the ACC after being projected to finish 11th. Collier won ACC Coach of the Year and will continue to lead the Yellow Jackets into the 2020 season.
