Bruce Heppler

Head coach of Tech's Golf Team

Bruce Heppler has had one of the most consistently successful careers of any Georgia Tech coach. Heppler hails from St. George, Utah and attended Brigham Young University for his undergraduate degree in accounting. He later attended the University of Massachusetts to attain his graduate degree in sports management.

He became the head coach in 1995 and since then has guided the Yellow Jackets to the NCAA Tournament every year since 1998. Since 1998, Georgia Tech has earned more automatic entries to the NCAA Tournament than any other school except Clemson. Additionally, the Georgia Tech team has performed outstandingly well in the classroom. They have earned a perfect academic progress report of 1000 for 13 straight years, and every single senior has graduated.

Heppler has won 13 ACC Conference Championships and has been named ACC Coach of the Year 10 times. He has set a new precedent for what it means to be a golfer at Tech and has created one of the most successful golf programs in the entire nation.
