The Reck

"1930 Ford Model A Sport Coupe"

Driving out on the field in the Reck

The first Ramblin' Reck was a 1914 Ford Model T owned by the Dean of Men, Professor Floyd Field, which he drove daily. The car became quite dilaidated, resulting in its current name. In the fall of 1960, Assistant Dean of Students Jim Dull discovered an immaculately restored black and gold 1930 Ford Model A Sport Coupe outside his dorm in Towers, the perfect vehicle to serve as the Institute's official Ramblin' Reck mascot

The car was owned by Ted Johnson, a pilot for Delta airlines, who sold the Model A in May of 1961 for $1000, an unbelievably low sum which he would later return to the Institute in 1983, making the car a true donation. In 1967, the car was entrusted solely to the Ramblin' Reck Club, including the election of one driver per year.

There have been three major restorations of the car, including a repainting transitioning from cheddar gold to its current colorscheme, a Lincoln gold handpicked by Bobby Dodd himself. The Ramblin' Reck has led the football team onto the field for every home game since 1961. The Reck bagan traveling far and wide as an ambassador of Tech spirit, including away football games for several seasons thereafter, traveling as far north as Michigan and as far west as El Paso

Today, the Reck can be seen making appearances around Georgia at weddings, parties, and other functions in order to raise necessary funds to provide adequate maintenance and to keep the Reck running for generations to come. More importantly, the Reck serves as a symbol for the spirit of Georgia Tech
