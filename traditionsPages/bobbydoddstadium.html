Bobby Dodd Stadium

"Welcome to the Flats"

Bobby Dodd Stadium at night

When Georgia Tech football began in 1892, the team did not have a field to play on. In 1905, Coach Heisman hired prisoners from the county jail to clear the land on the corner of North Avenue and Techwood Drive to create Tech's first athletic field, known as "The Flats." The students cam together and build the first stadium at Tech by constructing wooden risers with an overhang and fencing around the field.

In 1915, the Flats received new stands and was renamed Hugh Inman Grant Field with a gift from the Grant family. In 1988, the stadium was named Bobby Dodd Stadium at Historic Grant Field to honor coach Bobby Dodd. A statue remembering his service can be found on Callaway Plaza. Historic Grant Field is the oldest continuously used on-campus football field in the Football Bowl Subdivision (FBS)
