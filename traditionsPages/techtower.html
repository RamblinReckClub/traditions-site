Tech Tower

"The iconic building on Georgia Tech's campus"

Tech Tower was completed in 1888 and has remained an icon ever since. Originally, Tech Tower was called the "Academic Building," functioning as the school's only classroom building. The shot building next door, what is now the green area in Harrison Square, was where students would machine, build, and generally put into practice the things they learned in class.

In 1918, the freshman class added the "TECH" sign to the tower, giving the building its current nickname. Originally wood painted in white and gold, the "TECH" letters later had lights added to them, and eventually neon lights and metal frames were added as well. Now, Tech Tower houses the Registrar's Office and the Deans of the College of Engineering and College of Sciences
