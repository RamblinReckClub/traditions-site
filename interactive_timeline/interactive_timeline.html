  • 1885

    • October 13th:
    Georgia Tech is Founded

    The Georgia Legislature passes a bill appropriating $65,000 to found a technical college.

  • 1886

    • October 18th:
    Georgia Tech's home is determined

    After the commission to choose the location went through 24 ballots, Atlanta was finally chosen as the location of Georgia Tech.

  • 1888

    • May:
    Issac S. Hopkins appointed as first president of Georgia Tech

    He would serve until 1896 and in his time here, he serves as chair of Physics, Mandated stong ethics and an honor system, and created prepatory programs to increase enrollment.

    October:
    Georgia Tech opens

    The school opens for the Fall with 84 students and only one degree- Mechnical Engineering. William H. Glenn was the first student to register for classes.

  • 1892

    • Fall:
    The whistle comes to Tech

    It was installed in the 2nd shop building. Now it is operated by a computerized atomic clock to release the steam, and it blows to end classes and blows after touchdowns at home football games.

    November 5th:
    Georgia Tech starts playing football

    Professor Ernest West served as the first head coach and Tech lost to Mercer, Vanderbilt, and Auburn for the season.

  • 1893

    • November 4th:
    "Ramblin' Wreck" name is coined

    It originated from Tech students involved in a train crash on the way back from a 28-6 win against the boys over in Athens. This was also the first reference to white and gold in Tech history.

  • 1894

    • September 21st:
    Georgia Tech founds and joins the SIAA

    The SIAA (Southern Intercollegiate Athletic Association) was the first conference Tech was a part of, and they stayed with them until 1913.

  • 1896

    • June 24th:
    Lyman Hall appointed as 2nd president in Tech history

    He served until 1906 and while he was here he was the school's first mathematics chair, hired John Heisman as the team's football head coach, and added many degrees such as electrial, chemical, and textile engineering.

  • 1904

    • Fall:
    John Heisman becomes the Head Football Coach

    The legend served the position till 1919 and brought Tech football to prominence, and got us a national championship....His final record was 102-29-7.

  • 1905

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech adopts the moniker Yellow Jackets

    John Heisman wanted Tech to have one unified name to play under, and he chose the Yellow Jackets as that name.

  • 1906

    • June:
    Kenneth Matheson named 3rd president of Tech

    He served till 1922 and increased enrollment through advertising, established the Georgia Tech aviation school, and added an architecture department.

  • 1908

    • January 1st:
    ANAK Society is founded

    It was created first by 4 seniors. ANAK is a secret society at Tech that has (and still does) have a large influence on many things that happen on campus.

  • 1910

    • Unknown:
    IFC Dances were started

    Otherwise known as the Panhellenic Ball, these were the annual spring dances that were hosted at Tech until the 1950s

  • 1911

    • Unknown:
    Freshman Cake Race begun

    This started as an all cross country run open to all students, and has slowly expanded to become one of the most beloved homecoming traditions in Tech history.

  • 1913

    • Unknown:
    Women admitted to Tech's Evening School of Commerce

    While women would not be admitted to Tech's main school till 1952, the commerce school was ahead of the curve.

  • 1915

    • Unknown:
    RAT Caps were created

    This historic tradition that is still kept alive today was first founded here, and while the enforcement of RAT rules have hit the wayside, the caps are still distributed to freshman today. For more info, refer to the link here.

    Unknown:
    Golden Tornadoes nickname

    Tech, while still the Yellow Jackets, received the nickname the Golden Tornadoes, and this name would stick until the mid 1930s.

  • 1917

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech wins their 1st ever National Championship

    While Tech had an unclaimed national championship from 1916, 1917 was the first official accepted title Tech football had. This team is considered to be one of the greatest teams in not only Tech history, but NCAA history.
