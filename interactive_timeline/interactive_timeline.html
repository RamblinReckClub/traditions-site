  • 1885

    • October 13th:
    Georgia Tech is Founded

    The Georgia Legislature passes a bill appropriating $65,000 to found a technical college.

  • 1886

    • October 18th:
    Georgia Tech's home is determined

    After the commission to choose the location went through 24 ballots, Atlanta was finally chosen as the location of Georgia Tech.

  • 1888

    • May:
    Issac S. Hopkins appointed as first president of Georgia Tech

    He would serve until 1896 and in his time here, he serves as chair of Physics, Mandated stong ethics and an honor system, and created prepatory programs to increase enrollment.

    October:
    Georgia Tech opens

    The school opens for the Fall with 84 students and only one degree- Mechnical Engineering. William H. Glenn was the first student to register for classes. Tech Tower and the Shop Building were the only two buildings on campus. Only Tech Tower still stands to this day.

  • 1892

    • Fall:
    The whistle comes to Tech

    It was installed in the 2nd shop building. Now it is operated by a computerized atomic clock to release the steam, and it blows to end classes and blows after touchdowns at home football games.

    November 5th:
    Georgia Tech starts playing football

    Professor Ernest West served as the first head coach and Tech lost to Mercer, Vanderbilt, and Auburn for the season.

  • 1893

    • November 4th:
    "Ramblin' Wreck" name is coined

    It originated from Tech students involved in a train crash on the way back from a 28-6 win against the boys over in Athens. This was also the first reference to white and gold in Tech history.

  • 1894

    • September 21st:
    Georgia Tech founds and joins the SIAA

    The SIAA (Southern Intercollegiate Athletic Association) was the first conference Tech was a part of, and they stayed with them until 1913.

  • 1896

    • June 24th:
    Lyman Hall appointed as 2nd president in Tech history

    He served until 1906 and while he was here he was the school's first mathematics chair, hired John Heisman as the team's football head coach, and added many degrees such as electrial, chemical, and textile engineering.

  • 1904

    • Fall:
    John Heisman becomes the Head Football Coach

    The legend served the position till 1919 and brought Tech football to prominence, and got us a national championship....His final record was 102-29-7.

  • 1905

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech adopts the moniker Yellow Jackets

    John Heisman wanted Tech to have one unified name to play under, and he chose the Yellow Jackets as that name.

  • 1906

    • June:
    Kenneth Matheson named 3rd president of Tech

    He served till 1922 and increased enrollment through advertising, established the Georgia Tech aviation school, and added an architecture department.

  • 1908

    • January 1st:
    ANAK Society is founded

    It was created first by 4 seniors. ANAK is a secret society at Tech that has (and still does) have a large influence on many things that happen on campus.

  • 1910

    • Unknown:
    IFC Dances were started

    Otherwise known as the Panhellenic Ball, these were the annual spring dances that were hosted at Tech until the 1950s

  • 1911

    • Unknown:
    Freshman Cake Race begun

    This started as an all cross country run open to all students, and has slowly expanded to become one of the most beloved homecoming traditions in Tech history.

  • 1913

    • Unknown:
    Women admitted to Tech's Evening School of Commerce

    While women would not be admitted to Tech's main school till 1952, the commerce school was ahead of the curve.

  • 1915

    • Unknown:
    RAT Caps were created

    This historic tradition that is still kept alive today was first founded here, and while the enforcement of RAT rules have hit the wayside, the caps are still distributed to freshman today. For more info, refer to the link here.

    Unknown:
    Golden Tornadoes nickname

    Tech, while still the Yellow Jackets, received the nickname the Golden Tornadoes, and this name would stick until the mid 1930s.

  • 1916

    • October 7th:
    222-0

    Georgia Tech absolutely dominates the Cumberland Bulldogs 222-0 in the largest victory in college football hsitory. There were so many crazy events that happened, to learn more watch this video.

  • 1917

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech wins their 1st ever National Championship

    While Tech had an unclaimed national championship from 1916, 1917 was the first official accepted title Tech football had. This team is considered to be one of the greatest teams in not only Tech history, but NCAA history.

  • 1916

    • October 7th:
    222-0

    Georgia Tech absolutely dominates the Cumberland Bulldogs 222-0 in the largest victory in college football hsitory. There were so many crazy events that happened, to learn more watch this video.

  • 1920

    • June 7th:
    First Homecoming

    It was first sponsored by the GT Alumni Association and involved a barbeque on Grant Field, a baseball game between students and alumni, and a banquet at the Ansley Hotel.

    Unknown:
    William Alexander becomes Head Football Coach

    Another legend of Tech football coaching, he stayed for a long time till 1944 and retired with a record of 134-95-15.

  • 1922

    • Unknown:
    Marlon Brittain becomes 4th GT president

    He served till 1944 and was known for adding many more engineering degrees along with economics and social sciences, launched the Aerospace Engineering program at Tech, and prepared Tech for the US entry into WWII (including establishing a Navy ROTC program).

  • 1929

    • January 1st:
    Georgia Tech wins the 1929 Rose Bowl

    Tech went 9-0 in 1928 and won their 2nd national championship in football after this win. They won barely at a score of 8-7, but all that matters is getting the win.

    January:
    Stumpy's Bear comes to Campus

    Georgia Tech player Stump Thomason was given a bear named Bruin after the win in the Rose Bowl, and brought him back to Tech. Bruin lived under the Grant Field stands and was a menace to trash cans till Stumpy took him to Buffalo when he left Tech.

    Unknown:
    Old Ford Race created

    Also known as the Flyin Flivver race, this 70 mile race from Atlanta to Athens involved a mad dash in old cars and resulted in many crashes. This event would be shut down the following year for safety reasons, and was replaced in 1932 by the Wreck Parade.

  • 1930

    • Unknown:
    The Ramblin' Reck Club is founded

    Noticing a lack of student spirit during the great depression, coach William Alexander decided to start a spirit and tradition club which was called the Yellow Jacket Club. They would be rebranded in 1945 as the Ramblin' Reck Club.

  • 1933

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech joins the SEC

    Georgia Tech leaves the conferences it used to play with and officially joins the Southeastern Conference to play it's sports....And it would stay there till 1963.

  • 1938

    • Unknown:
    Drownproofing is introduced

    Swimming and Diving coach Freddy Lanoue created drownproofing, and it became mandatory for all Tech students to graduate from 1940 till 1986. Coach McAuley would take over teaching drownproofing till its final discontinuation. To see more info on this, watch this video.

  • 1944

    • Unknown:
    Blake Van Leer becomes Tech's 5th president

    He served till 1956 and in his time became the first engineer to become a Tech president, establish the Industrial Engineering department, created a PHD for engineering, and facilitated the integration of women into degree programs at Tech.

  • 1945

    • March:
    Sideways the Dog shows up on campus

    Sideways was a white terrier with black patches on her face, and she arrived at Tech after being thrown out of a car window and getting injured. She was cared for, and soon became a member of the school: Going to classes, leading the team onto the field, etc...Now, student leave pennies on her grave for good luck on Tests.

    Unknown:
    Bobby Dodd becomes Head Football Coach

    The winningest Tech football coach served here till 1966 (before become an Athletic Director for Tech) and led Tech to some national championships and a record of 165-64-8.

  • 1948

    • Unknown:
    School's name becomes Georgia Institute of Technology

    The school's name was officially changed to this to reflect the school's focus on technological and scientific research.

    Unknown:
    Junior's Grill opens up

    Junior's Grill is opened up by Wilbur Gold Jr. as Pilgrim's, and it would soon turn into a popular eating spot at Tech and run by Tommy Klemins. However Junior's Grill was taken away some time later due to a lack of business and was replaced by Highland Bakery.

  • 1952

    • Unknown:
    Women students are enrolled at Tech

    While females had participated in the School of Commerce some time ago, these are the first women to actually participate in a degree program at Tech.

  • 1956

    • January 1st:
    Sugar Bowl Rebellion

    Georgia Tech was supposed to play Pittsburgh, but Pittsburgh had a black player. The governor forbade them from playing the game for racial reasons, but students rebelled, Van Leer stood his ground and refused to let the racial politics take over, and Tech was allowed to play the game. They ended up winning 7-0 and showed Tech stood out in Southern schools in being against racial inequality.

  • 1957

    • Unknown:
    Edwin Harrison becomes Tech's 6th president

    He served till 1968 and in his time expanded campus, facilitated the racial integration of Georgia Tech, oversaw the construction of buildings such as the Physics Building, and helped establish the school of information and computer science, the first in the country.

  • 1960

    • Unknown:
    Clifford the Lion

    Football player Joe Auer brought Clifford to come and live on campus in Cloudman dormitories. However, after numerous growling incidents and students fearing for their lives (who could have guessed?), he had to be removed.

  • 1961

    • Unknown:
    The first African-American students are admitted

    Georgia Tech became the first university in the Deep South to allow African-American students to enroll to the school without a court order. These brave pioneers are honored with statues on campus.

    Unknown:
    The Reck is acquired

    After years of searching for an older Ford car to serve as the mascot of the school, Dean Dull found a 1930 Ford Model A Sport Coupe made by Delta Pilot Ted Johnson. Dull bought the car for $1000 and soon became an icon for the university.

    September 30:
    The Reck first drives onto the field

    This is the first of many many games where the Reck lead the team onto the field before the start of football games. The Reck became an icon quickly as Tech ended up winning the game against Rice by a score of 24-0.

  • 1964

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech leaves the SEC

    After many conflicts between Bobby Dodd and Bear Bryant (coach of Alabama), and Dodd's issue with SEC recruiting practices, Georgia Tech leaves the Southeastern Conference and becomes independent.

  • 1967

    • Unknown:
    Bud Carson becomes the Head Football Coach

    He remained coach until 1971, but with a record of 27-27, he was soon fired and became the first Georgia Tech head football coach to be fired.

  • 1969

    • Unknown:
    Mini 500 joins homecoming

    The Mini 500 is a tricycle race around Peters Parking Deck. It was inspired by the hazing Tech students did on Freshman fraternity pledges, making them ride tricycles around campus.

    Unknown:
    The T is stolen for the first time

    The group known as "The Magnificent Seven" stole the east side T on Tech Tower to commemorate the retirement of President Edwin Harrison and presented it to him. This was the start of a long tradition and obsession with trying to steal the T on Tech Tower.

    Unknown:
    Arthur Hansen becomes the 7th Tech President

    Hansen only served as president till 1971, but in his time he acquired land on West Campus, opened the Wenn Student Center, and oversaw the opening of the first women's dorm: Fulmer Hall.

  • 1972

    • Unknown:
    Joseph Pettit becomes Tech's 8th president

    He served till 1986 and pushed hard to make Tech a research school by promoting PHD research, overseeing the transition of the Engineering Experimental Station to the Georgia Tech Research Institute, and helped establish the Office of Minority Educational Development.

    Unknown:
    First appearance of Buzz

    Judy McNair created and donned a homemade yellowjacket costume and wore it to all the home football games and it was huge hit with fans. However, it wouldn't be till 1980 till Buzz officially ingrained himself on campus.

  • 1973

    • May 26th:
    First run of the George C Griffin Pi Mile

    56 students ran the first race nad is the 2nd longest continuing run race in Atlanta. The 3 mile race (which is now 3.14) was won by Scott Stewart.

  • 1974

    • Unknown:
    Women's basketball established

    Women's basketball was the first ever Women's Varsity Sport established at Georgia Tech.

  • 1978

    • Unknown:
    Georgia Tech joins the ACC

    After being independent, Georgia Tech finally joins a conference, specifically the ACC (Atlantic Coast Conference) and they have remained in that sports conference to this date.

  • 1980

    • October 11:
    Buzz makes his first official appearance

    After McNair's unofficial costume, Richie Bland paid to have a yellow jacket costume designed and wore it to a pep rally. Rather than getting permission, he would simply sneak onto the field, and became such a huge hit, that the mascot would stay.

  • 1987

    • Unknown:
    John Patrick Crecine becomes Tech's 9th president

    He would serve till 1992 and was very succesful. He helped create 3 new schools (College of Computing, College of Management, and College of Liberal Arts), led Tech to help Atlanta win its bid for the 1996 Summer Olympics, and built many structures for the Olympics such as West Campus dorms and the Aquatic center.

  • 1989

    • Unknown:
    T-Night

    This event was done to celebrate the first week of classes and consists of festival, games, food, and presentation on Georgia Tech traditions.

  • 1991

    • January 1st:
    Georgia Tech Football wins its 4th National Championship

    Under Coach Bobby Ross, Tech went 11-0-1 in the 1990 season and came up with a shared national championship with the University of Colorado. A win in the citrus bowl against Nebraska on this date cemented Tech's championship win.

  • 1994

    • Unknown:
    G Wayne Clough becomes 10th Tech President

    He served until 2008 and he became the first Tech alumnus to be named Tech president. Under his time, Tech's academic reach expanded to locations across the world, research expenditures doubled, and Tech Square opened, expanding campus across the Downtown Connector.

  • 1996

    • July 19th:
    Atlanta hosts the 1996 Olympics

    Georgia Tech's campus served as the home of the Olympic village, and had venues that hosted Olympic events such as the McAuley Aquatic Center. The Kessler Campanile was also constructed for these games.

  • 2007

    • May 22nd:
    GT Women's Tennis wins National Title

    This is Georgia Tech's first and only NCAA national title. They were coached by Bryan Shelton And won it on a 4-2 win over UCLA in Athens, Georgia.

  • 2008

    • Unknown:
    Ramblin' Reck Club take over T-Book

    Physical copies of the T-Book had stopped printing in 1970 due to lack of funding. But starting this year, the Ramblin Reck Club brought back the physical copies and handed them out to the entering class at freshman convocation.

    Unknown:
    Paul Johnson becomes Tech's Football Head Coach

    He brought forward a new era in GT football: The triple option. Under his reign, Tech has a unique offense that provided for many upsets and lots of success. He served until 2018 and finished with a record of 83-60.

  • 2008

    • Unknown:
    G.P. "Bud" Peterson becomes Tech's 11th President

    He served until 2019 and helped establish many student programs (such as CARE and Grand Challenges), oversaw a near tripling in admission applications, creation of many buildings such as the Clough Undergraduate Learning Commons, and led the growth of Tech square with innovation and research.

  • 2019

    • Unknown:
    Geoff Collins becomes Tech's Football Coach

    Coming from Temple, he had to oversee a change in the school's system from triple option to a more normal offense, and brought together a complete rebranding of the school's football team. He is the current head coach of the team.

    Unknown:
    Angel Cabrera becomes Tech's 12th president

    He is the current Tech president and the first Spanish-born president of an American University. While still new to his presidency, he has already had to deal with many challenges, such as guiding Tech through the COVID-19 Pandemic.
