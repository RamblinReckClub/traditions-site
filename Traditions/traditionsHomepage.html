Traditions at Tech

Summary of GT Traditions

Georgia Tech is full of traditions and history that make the school special. From the various homecoming events to our beloved mascots, there are so many things that Georgia Tech has to offer.

Some of the traditions such as the Freshman Cake Race date back to as early as 1911 and have continued every year since. Other traditions such as the Mini 500 and the Ramblin’ Wreck Parade demonstrate the creativity of students and highlight the “work hard, play hard” motto that so many students embody.

Georgia Tech’s campus is also full of beloved buildings and sounds such as Tech Tower, the horn of the Ramblin’ Reck, and the Whistle that blows everyday weekday between classes. These are sights and sounds that every student is familiar with and make our campus feel like home.
