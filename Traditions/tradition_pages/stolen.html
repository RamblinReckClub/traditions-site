Stealing the "T"

"The not so official tradition on campus"

Tech Tower with a missing "T".

To commemorate President Edwin D. Harrison's retirement in 1969, a group of students calling themselves "The Magnificent Seven" stole the "T" from the east side of Tech Tower. The "T" was then presented to Harrison as a gift a couple days later in celebration of his retirement. According to tradition, the specific "T" to be stolen is the one facing the Downtown Connector. If that "T" has already been removed, then the next "T" in a clockwise direction is the target. After all of the "T"s have been stolen, the next letters to be removed are the "H"s, and after the "E"s, and finally the "C"s.

When a student died while climbing the Alexander Coliseum (now MCCamish Pavilion) in 1999, stealing the "T" from Tech Tower or scaling any institute building was prohibited for students' safety, and security measures were put up to deter theft. The tradition marks the history of stealing the "T" from Tech Tower and is not excuse for vandalizing campus property.
